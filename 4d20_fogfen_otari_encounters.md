# 4D20 RANDOM ENCOUNTER TABLE — FOGFEN TO OTARI
**Bell Curve Distribution (4d20 = 4-80)**
**Setting:** Travel from Gauntlight Keep through Fogfen swamp to Otari town

---

- **4-9**: DEADLY (Level 5 creatures/hazards, major rewards)
- **10-19**: DIFFICULT (Level 4 creatures/hazards)
- **20-29**: MODERATE (Level 3 creatures/hazards)
- **30-38**: EASY (Level 2 creatures/hazards, minor rewards)
- **39-41**: TRIVIAL (Flavor encounters, travelers, descriptions)
- **42**: NO EVENT (Peak of bell curve)
- **43-45**: TRIVIAL (Flavor encounters, travelers, descriptions)

## 4-9 — DEADLY ENCOUNTERS (Level 5, Far Tail)

### 4: THE ANCIENT GUARDIAN
**Setup:** Deep in Fogfen where the mist is thickest and oldest.

**Read-Aloud:** The fog parts to reveal a massive shape—bark-covered limbs creak as ancient eyes
 open. This treant has stood watch for centuries.

**Creature:** Treant (Level 5, Uncommon - but fits setting)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=388

**GM Notes:** The treant is territorial but not immediately hostile. It demands to know why the party disturbs the Fogfen. Can be negotiated with (Diplomacy DC 22) or fought.

**Development:** If befriended, the treant warns of Gauntlight's evil and offers a blessing (+1 circumstance bonus to saves vs undead for 24 hours). If defeated, its heartwood contains rare reagents worth (5 + 2×level) gp.

---

### 5: THE DROWNED ONES
**Setup:** A flooded section of the Gaunt Trail where water pools unnaturally.

**Read-Aloud:** Waterlogged corpses rise from the murky pools, their movements jerky and purposeful. The Gauntlight's influence has reached even here.

**Creature:** 2x Zombie Brute (Level 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=424

**GM Notes:** Two zombie brutes attack simultaneously. They fight mindlessly until destroyed. The water is difficult terrain.

**Development:** Among the corpses' belongings: a waterlogged journal from an Otari fisher who went missing weeks ago, and a pouch with (3 + level) gp.

---

### 6: WILL-O'-WISP AMBUSH
**Setup:** Eerie lights dance through the fog ahead.

**Read-Aloud:** Beautiful lights pulse and weave through the mist, beckoning you forward. Then you realize—they're circling, hunting.

**Creature:** Will-o'-Wisp (Level 6, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=414

**GM Notes:** The wisp tries to lead the party into hazards (quicksand, deep water) before attacking. Uses hit-and-run tactics, feeding on fear.

**Development:** If defeated, its essence dissipates but leaves behind a glowing residue that can be harvested (Arcana DC 20) worth (8 + 2×level) gp to wizards, or can be used as material component for darkvision spell.

---

### 7: ANDOREN DESERTERS
**Setup:** A makeshift barricade blocks the trail, manned by hardened criminals.

**Read-Aloud:** "That's far enough, travelers." The leader's tattered uniform bears the eagle of Andoran, now stained and torn. "This here's a toll road now. Your coin or your lives."

**Creature:** Bandit Captain (Level 4, Common) + 2x Bandit (Level 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/NPCs.aspx?ID=889  
https://2e.aonprd.com/NPCs.aspx?ID=888

**GM Notes:** Former Andoren soldiers who deserted after a failed campaign. Captain is smart and will negotiate. Wants (2×level) gp per person. If refused, attacks. Bandits flee if captain falls.

**Development:** Captain carries a wanted poster from Almas (Andoran's capital) offering (10 + 2×level) gp reward for his capture, and a letter from family in Augustana begging him to come home. Map shows other deserter camps in Immenwood.

---

### 8: QUICKSAND TRAP
**Setup:** The trail narrows between two pools of stagnant water.

**Read-Aloud:** The ground beneath your feet suddenly gives way, pulling you down into cold, sucking mud.

**Hazard:** Quicksand (Hazard 3, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=10

**GM Notes:** DC 20 Reflex save or sink. Requires Athletics checks to escape. Party members can help with ropes.

**Development:** Once past, a DC 18 Perception check reveals a skeleton half-buried nearby with a corset knife, an everburning torch, and (4 + level) gp in a waterproof pouch.

---

### 9: THE CORPSELIGHT SWARM
**Setup:** Multiple pale flames drift through the fog, converging on your position.

**Read-Aloud:** Ghostly lights multiply in the mist—dozens of them, each pulsing with malevolent hunger.

**Creature:** 3x Flickerwisp (Level 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=1036

**GM Notes:** Three flickerwisps attack in coordination. They target spellcasters first. Vulnerable to area effects.

**Development:** Their destruction causes the fog to thin temporarily, revealing a shortcut that saves 1 hour of travel and a small shrine to Desna (goddess of travel and luck) with (6 + level) gp in offerings.

## 10-19 — DIFFICULT ENCOUNTERS (Level 4)

### 10: THE MARSH GIANT
**Setup:** Heavy footsteps shake the ground. Something massive approaches through the reeds.

**Read-Aloud:** A towering figure emerges from the fog, dragging a crude club. Its skin is mottled green-gray, covered in moss and algae.

**Creature:** Ogre Warrior (Level 3, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=317

**GM Notes:** This ogre has adapted to swamp life. Uses terrain advantage, trying to push PCs into water. Fights to the death.

**Development:** Its lair is nearby (DC 18 Survival to find) containing (8 + 3×level) gp in stolen goods and a barrel of Otari ale.

---

### 11: VENOMOUS SERPENT
**Setup:** A massive snake coils around a dead tree, watching your approach.

**Read-Aloud:** Scales glisten wetly as the serpent uncoils, easily twenty feet long. Its tongue tastes the air, and it hisses.

**Creature:** Giant Viper (Level 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=382

**GM Notes:** The viper strikes from ambush if possible. Its venom is potent. Flees if reduced below half HP.

**Development:** The snake was guarding a nest. Inside: 2 doses of giant viper venom (worth (3 + level) gp each) and shed skin (crafting material worth (2 + level) gp).

---

### 12: UNDEAD PATROL
**Setup:** Shambling figures move through the mist in formation—a grim echo of their living days.

**Read-Aloud:** Four skeletal warriors march in lockstep, rusted weapons at the ready. The Gauntlight's power animates them still.

**Creature:** 4x Skeleton Guard (Level -1, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=372

**GM Notes:** These were Gauntlight guards centuries ago. Fight in formation. Vulnerable to bludgeoning damage.

**Development:** Their armor bears the crest of Belcorra Haruvex. One carries a key to a locked door in Gauntlight and (5 + level) gp in ancient Chelish coins.

---

### 13: POISONOUS FOG BANK
**Setup:** The fog ahead takes on a sickly yellow-green hue.

**Read-Aloud:** Your eyes water and throat burns as you enter the discolored mist. This isn't natural fog.

**Hazard:** Poisonous Fog (Hazard 4, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=9

**GM Notes:** Fortitude DC 21 or become sickened 2. Lasts for 100 feet of travel. Can be detected with Survival DC 18.

**Development:** The fog is caused by rotting vegetation. A DC 20 Nature check identifies medicinal herbs growing here worth (4 + 2×level) gp.

---

### 14: THE GHOUL PACK
**Setup:** Hunched figures crouch over something in the trail ahead.

**Read-Aloud:** Gray-skinned humanoids look up from their meal—a dead deer. Their eyes gleam with hunger as they notice fresher prey.

**Creature:** 2x Ghoul (Level 1, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=218

**GM Notes:** Two ghouls attack together, trying to paralyze targets. Fight until one dies, then the survivor flees.

**Development:** The deer carcass can be salvaged for rations. Nearby, a DC 17 Perception check finds a ghoul lair with (5 + 2×level) gp in coins and a holy symbol of Pharasma (goddess of death) - the ghouls' former victim was a traveling priest.

---

### 15: COLLAPSING BRIDGE
**Setup:** An old wooden bridge spans a deep, murky channel.

**Read-Aloud:** The bridge groans ominously under your weight. Rotten planks crack and splinter.

**Hazard:** Collapsing Bridge (Hazard 3, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=5

**GM Notes:** Reflex DC 20 or fall 15 feet into cold water with submerged debris. Athletics DC 15 to climb out.

**Development:** In the water below, a DC 19 Perception check spots a sunken chest containing a lesser healing potion and (6 + 3×level) gp.

---

### 16: TERRITORIAL BOAR
**Setup:** Snorting and crashing sounds come from the underbrush.

**Read-Aloud:** A massive boar charges from the reeds, tusks lowered, eyes wild with territorial fury.

**Creature:** Boar (Level 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=52

**GM Notes:** The boar is protecting its young nearby. Can be calmed with Nature DC 18 or scared off with intimidation.

**Development:** If defeated, provides 20 pounds of quality meat. If spared, the party finds its den with 3 boar piglets (could be sold in Otari for (2 + level) gp each).

---

### 17: SMUGGLER'S CACHE
**Setup:** A suspicious pile of rocks near the trail.

**Read-Aloud:** Something about these rocks seems deliberately placed. Closer inspection reveals they're hiding something.

**Hazard:** Alarm Trap (Hazard 2, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=1

**GM Notes:** Thievery DC 18 to disarm. If triggered, alerts bandits in area (roll another encounter in 10 minutes).

**Development:** Behind the rocks: a smuggler's cache with (7 + 3×level) gp, 1 potion of healing (minor), and a coded ledger written in Qadiran (leads to spice smuggling ring from Katheer if investigated).

---

### 18: THE TALDAN KNIGHT'S CURSE
**Setup:** A figure in ancient armor stalks through the fog with purpose.

**Read-Aloud:** Dead eyes lock onto you from beneath a rusted helm bearing the lion of Taldor. This undead warrior moves with terrible intelligence, muttering prayers to Aroden, the dead god.

**Creature:** Wight (Level 3, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=413

**GM Notes:** This wight was a Taldan knight who died seeking Aroden's blessing at Gauntlight before the god's death. Fights tactically, uses Drain Life aggressively. Cries out "For Taldor! For Aroden!" in combat.

**Development:** Wears a Taldan knight's signet ring (worth (3 + level) gp, or can be returned to Otari for (5 + 2×level) gp reward and reputation gain). Carries a faded letter from Oppara, Taldor's capital, dated centuries ago.

---

### 19: SINKING MUD PIT
**Setup:** The trail becomes increasingly soft and waterlogged.

**Read-Aloud:** Your boots sink deep into the mud with each step. Too late, you realize this entire section is unstable.

**Hazard:** Sinking Mud (Hazard 3, Common)  
https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=10

**GM Notes:** Athletics DC 18 to avoid becoming stuck. Requires multiple checks to escape. Party can use ropes and teamwork.

**Development:** A skeleton trapped in the mud holds a scroll of water breathing, (2 + level) gp, and a map marking safe paths through Fogfen with notes in Varisian.

## 39-41 — TRIVIAL ENCOUNTERS (Flavor, No Combat)

### 40: BEAUTIFUL VISTA
**Setup:** The fog parts momentarily.

**Read-Aloud:** For a brief moment, the fog clears. You see Otari below—the bay sparkling in sunlight, fishing boats dotting the water, smoke rising from chimneys. It's beautiful and peaceful, a stark contrast to the gloomy Fogfen.

**Scenic Moment**  
No combat

**GM Notes:** A moment of peace and beauty. Party can rest here safely for 10 minutes to recover.

**Development:** The vista provides hope and motivation. Each PC gains a +1 circumstance bonus to their next saving throw (lasts until used).

---

### 41: CAYDEN'S LUCK
**Setup:** A perfect circle of glowing mushrooms blocks the path.

**Read-Aloud:** Luminescent mushrooms form a perfect circle, pulsing with soft blue light. Nearby, an empty ale mug sits on a stone - someone left an offering here. The air feels charged with good fortune.

**Magical Phenomenon**  
No combat

**GM Notes:** Fey circle blessed by followers of Cayden Cailean (god of freedom, ale, and bravery). Nature DC 16 or Religion DC 18 identifies it as a shrine where travelers leave offerings for safe passage.

**Development:** If party leaves an offering (any drink or (1×level) gp), they receive Cayden's blessing: +1 circumstance bonus to next attack roll or saving throw. If disturbed without offering, minor bad luck (disadvantage on next check). A small plaque reads "Drink deep, travel far - Cayden watches."

## USAGE NOTES

### Rolling the Dice
- Roll 4d20 and sum the result (4-80)
- Roll once per 2 hours of travel, or when dramatically appropriate
- GM can adjust frequency based on pacing needs

### Scaling for Party Level
- **Level 1-2 Party**: Reduce creature HP by 20%, reduce DCs by 2
- **Level 3-4 Party**: Use as written
- **Level 5+ Party**: Increase creature HP by 20%, increase DCs by 2, add additional creatures

### Environmental Factors
- **Gauntlight Pulse**: When lighthouse glows, roll with disadvantage (use lower result) - more undead
- **Heavy Fog**: Perception checks at -2, stealth checks at +2
- **Night Travel**: Double encounter frequency, undead more common
- **Rainy Weather**: Hazards more dangerous (+2 DC), but undead less active

### Connecting Encounters
- Repeated similar results suggest a pattern (ghoul pack hunting, bandit patrols, etc.)
- Use failed encounters to foreshadow future threats
- Survivors can return with reinforcements

### Rewards Summary by Tier
- **Trivial (39-45)**: Roleplay, information, minor blessings
- **Easy (30-38, 46-54)**: 10-40gp, basic items, minor potions
- **Moderate (20-29, 55-64)**: 40-80gp, +1 runes, moderate potions
- **Difficult (10-19, 65-74)**: 80-150gp, +1 items, plot hooks
- **Deadly (4-9, 75-80)**: 150-250gp, multiple +1 items, major plot information

### Otari Connections
- Many encounters reference Otari NPCs, locations, and factions
- Returning items/information to Otari builds reputation
- Reputation in Otari unlocks discounts, allies, and plot hooks
- Otari guard pays bounties for bandit intelligence and monster threats

### Campaign Integration
- Use encounters to foreshadow Gauntlight threats
- Bandit plots can tie to corrupt officials
- Undead activity increases as Gauntlight grows stronger
- Survivors' stories spread through Otari, affecting party reputation
